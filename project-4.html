Southern Water Monitor

Autonomous IoT Water Tank Monitoring & Pump Control System
ESP32 IoT BLE WiFi Ultrasonic Sensor Solid State Relay Home Automation

1. Project Overview

The Southern Water Monitor is a market-ready IoT product developed entirely in-house. The device autonomously monitors residential and commercial water tank levels, controls pump operation via a solid-state relay, and delivers real-time data to a web dashboard — all configurable wirelessly via Bluetooth Low Energy. This system eliminates the manual effort of monitoring water tank levels and manually operating water pumps.

AUTO Mode

The microcontroller continuously monitors the tank level and switches the pump ON when the level falls below the configured start threshold, and OFF when it reaches the stop threshold. Both thresholds are set via the mobile application.

MANUAL Mode

The internal DPDT switch physically bypasses the relay, routing mains power directly through the DP switch to the motor. The microcontroller is completely removed from the circuit, ensuring full operator control even in case of device failure.

5. Future Development Roadmap

The following enhancements are proposed for subsequent product revisions, ordered by implementation priority and commercial impact.

F1: Wireless Mode Selection

Elimination of physical DPDT switch. Once the optional cloud server infrastructure is deployed, the internal DPDT hard switch can be replaced with a software-controlled relay or MOSFET circuit. The AUTO/MANUAL mode transition would then be managed entirely through the dashboard or mobile application over the network.

F2: Cloud Server Deployment

Migrate the Node.js dashboard from a local PC to a Raspberry Pi server. Devices register automatically over the internet via a polling model, eliminating the requirement for the client and server to be on the same network. This enables centralised management of multiple installations across different sites from a single dashboard.

F3: Custom PCB Design & IP65 Enclosure

Design a custom PCB to replace the prototype board, reducing assembly time and improving reliability. Source an IP65-rated enclosure for outdoor installations.

F4: Flutter Mobile Application

Develop the companion mobile application using Flutter to replace the manual nRF Connect BLE configuration process. The app would provide a guided setup wizard, live level gauge, pump control, push notifications for tank empty/overflow events, and historical usage graphs.

F5: Water Quality Sensing & Flow Metering

Integration of turbidity, pH, and flow rate sensors to expand the product into a comprehensive water management platform. This positions Southern IoT for industrial and agricultural market segments with significantly higher average selling prices.

6. Conclusion

The Southern Water Monitor represents a commercially viable, technically complete IoT product that can be brought to market with minimal additional investment. The prototype is fully functional, the firmware is stable, and the dashboard infrastructure is operational. With a manufacturing cost of approximately $32 per unit and a recommended retail price of $85, the product offers a strong 62% gross margin with clear pathways to improvement through bulk procurement and PCB optimisation.

It is recommended that a pilot production run of 20–30 units be initiated for market validation, targeted at residential customers and small commercial installations. Concurrent development of the mobile application and cloud infrastructure will position Southern IoT for scalable growth in the smart water management segment.
